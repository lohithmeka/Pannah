OUR WORK

A Family-Based Pathway
to Learning and Livelihoods

Panaah transforms entire families by rebuilding how children learn and how youth earn.

Across our community centers, every learner’s journey — child, teenager or parent — is guided through a continuum of learning-to-livelihood programs that move families from survival to growth.

We call this the Panaah Pathway.

Each program is deeply rooted in the community, designed to ensure that children stay in school, teenagers thrive in careers, and families rise together.

GRADE 5–10

Panaah Solvers

Solvers are children who are at-risk of dropping out or falling behind.

Many come from households with unstable incomes, unsafe environments, and limited access to quality schooling. At Panaah, we rebuild their confidence by bridging academics with real-world relevance.

The Two Anchors of Solvers:

Academic Mastery

  • Intensive support in foundational literacy, numeracy, and marathi.
  • Diagnostic assessments every term to track grade-level improvement.
  • Bridge courses and weekend learning labs for remediation.

Community Projects

  • Hands-on, problem-based projects that turn learning into action.
  • Children research local issues — waste, sanitation, food, safety — and design solutions.
  • Builds teamwork, communication, and civic responsibility.
Goal

Every Solver stays in school, reaches grade-level mastery, and builds the mindset of a problem-solver.

Outcome

Children who were once disengaged become confident learners and contributors to their community.

Future Path

Solvers who show academic promise move into the Scholars track.

“From high-need classrooms
to top 5% universities.”

GRADE 5–10

Panaah Scholars

Scholars are students who show exceptional curiosity, discipline and potential for advanced learning.

They are nurtured through a rigorous and personalized track that prepares them to compete at national and international levels—bridging the world’s best opportunities to their communities.

What We Do:

Provide specialized mentoring and test preparation for competitive exams such as:

  • SOF Olympiads (IMO, NSO, NCO, IEO)
  • Aryabhatta Math Test
  • Vikram Sarabhai Science Exam, Allen Talent Test
  • Jawanova Divya Dhar Exam
  • Akshara Scholarship School Scholarship
  • and other State/Corporate Scholarship Exams.
  • Offer access to advanced STEM learning, research-based projects, and Olympiad labs.
  • Facilitate exchange programs with institutions and mentors across India and abroad.
  • Conduct mock exams, study circles and high-rigor weekend sessions.
Goal

Enable our top 10% learners to reach the top 5% universities in India and across the world.

Outcome

Over 50 students have ranked nationally in Olympiads and scholarships.

Future Path

After completing Grade 10, Scholars transition into Panaah Professionals for focused career tracks.

“From classrooms to careers,
from dreams to dignity.”

GRADE 11- FIRST JOB

Panaah Professionals

Professionals are teenagers and young adults who are ready to translate their learning into livelihoods.

This program creates bridges from high-need communities to the industries shaping tomorrow’s economy.

Current Career Streams:

Entrepreneur-ship

Incubation-based learning where youth identify local problems and design low-cost, scalable businesses.

Filmmaking & Media

A powerful storytelling program where teens learn to script, shoot, and produce short films amplifying community voices.

Technology & AI

Training in digital skills, coding, data handling, and emerging AI tools for real-world problem-solving.

Teaching & Learning Support

Creating the next generation of community educators.

Upcoming 2025–26

Creative Community Services

Building pathways in design, storytelling, and local enterprises.

Upcoming 2025–26

Each stream combines 10 hours of weekly learning (technical + soft skills) with field projects, mentor sessions, and internships.

After graduation, youth are connected to employment or business opportunities that earn ₹45,000–₹50,000 per month, creating a 4–5x increase in family income.

Goal

Equip teenagers for growth jobs and entrepreneurship pathways that lift entire families out of poverty.

Outcome

Families move from surviving to thriving, with sustained upward income mobility and long-term agency.

The Panaah Pathway in Motion

Imagine three concentric rings radiating
outward from one center:

Children (Solvers)
build confidence and learning foundations.

Children (Scholars)
accelerate excellence and prepare for higher education.

Teenagers (Professionals)

At the core of every ring are parents and local women leaders, who run the centers, manage budgets, and serve as living proof that change grows from within.

Together, we are building a self-sustaining, community-led model of urban transformation— where every family learns, earns, and leads.
